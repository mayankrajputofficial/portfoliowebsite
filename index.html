01About

I'm a Cloud & DevOps Engineer building and running production systems on Oracle Cloud Infrastructure. After leaving a corporate job, I made a deliberate pivot into infrastructure and automation — learning by doing, not just watching tutorials.

My current setup: a self-hosted portfolio running on an OCI Always Free VM (1 CPU, 1 GB RAM) — containerised with Docker, served by Nginx, deployed automatically via GitHub Actions CI/CD. Every piece of the stack is something I debugged, fixed, and understood — from iptables rules to SSH key permissions.

I document everything I learn on my blog — real debugging stories, real infrastructure lessons. No fluff. Actively working toward OCI certifications and deeper Kubernetes & Terraform experience.

01Who I Am

Outside the terminal, I'm someone who believes the best way to understand the world is to move through it. I ride bikes on empty highways at sunrise, lift weights before the city wakes up, and chase light with a camera in places most people don't think to stop.

I'm as obsessive about gadgets and new technology as I am about cloud infrastructure — probably more so. If there's a new phone, a new car, a new piece of kit that does something interesting, I want to understand how it works and whether it's actually good or just marketed well.

Everything on this page — the photos, the stories, the writing — is real. No aesthetic curation. Just what actually happened.
